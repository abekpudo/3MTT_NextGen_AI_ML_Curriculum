 NLP Capstone Design: Solving Language Problems

 1. Overview
The Month 3 Capstone is your chance to specialize. NLP is a broad field, so your project should focus on one core task (Classification, Retrieval, or Generation) and solve a problem that is relevant to your community or industry.

 2. Choosing Your Specialization

 2.1. Classification Track
- Project Idea: An automated "Fake News" or "Misinformation" detector for local news headlines.
- Goal: Classify text as Reliable vs. Unreliable.

 2.2. Retrieval Track (Search & QA)
- Project Idea: A "Knowledge Assistant" for a specific domain (e.g., Nigerian Constitutional Law or Medical First Aid).
- Goal: Use Semantic Search to find the right answer in a document database.

 2.3. Generative Track (Summarization)
- Project Idea: A "Daily News Condenser" that takes long articles and produces a 3-sentence summary in Pidgin or English.
- Goal: Use a Seq2Seq model (like T5 or BART) for summarization.

 3. The Design Checklist
Before starting, ensure your project design covers:
1. The "Why": What problem does this solve?
2. The "Data": Where will you get at least 1,000 text samples?
3. The "Baseline": What is the simplest way to solve this? (e.g., TF-IDF + Naive Bayes).
4. The "SOTA": Which Transformer model will you use to improve results? (e.g., BERT, RoBERTa, T5).
5. The "Safety": How will you handle hallucinations or biases?

 4. Measuring Success
You must define two types of metrics:
- Technical Metrics: F1-Score, ROC-AUC, BLEU, or ROUGE.
- Operational Metrics: Latency (how fast is the model?) and Model Size.

 5. Ethical Considerations in NLP
- Language Bias: Ensure your model doesn't favor one dialect or socio-economic group.
- Privacy: If you use real customer data, you MUST anonymize it.
- Harmful Content: Does your model generate or propagate hate speech?
