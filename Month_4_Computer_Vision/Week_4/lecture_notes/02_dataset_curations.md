Dataset Curations

Overview
Review dataset curation practices specific to vision projects, including labeling, augmentation, balancing, and ethical considerations.

Why This Topic Matters
Curating a high-quality vision dataset is critical for model performance and fairness, especially when real-world images can include bias and noise.

Lecture Goals
- Collect and organize images for the task
- Define annotation schemas and quality checks
- Balance classes and avoid label leakage
- Document dataset provenance and usage rights
- Prepare data augmentation and partitioning strategies

Key Concepts
- annotation
- balancing
- dataset ethics
- provenance
- partitioning

Classroom Example / Case Study
- Explain how to manage image metadata and labels consistently
- Discuss the importance of validating annotation quality
- Show how to split images into train/validation/test sets without leakage

Practical Implementation Notes
- Respect dataset licenses and privacy requirements.
- Monitor class balance and performance across groups.
- Keep raw and processed datasets organized separately.
